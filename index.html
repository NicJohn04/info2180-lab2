"Oh Ocean"

October 01, 2024 by Nicole Johnson

Oh how much I love the ocean,
The soothing sounds and and stunning view,
That salty scent wrapped all around me,
Oh wonderful Ocean, I love you!

Oh how much I love the ocean,
And I love the beach its waters meet!
Gallivanting with friends in sand and sun,
As the ocean laps around our feet.

Oh how much I loathe the ocean,
With its wicked currents, its thrashing waves
And all the creatures waiting to snatch me-
Oh foul Ocean, spare me this day.

Oh how much I love the ocean,
Even the storms when they pass through.
I watch from afar in awe and wonder
As I find beauty in the danger too.

I wrote this poem while reminiscing on my holiday in the Bahamas. Now I know what you might be thinking, why would I leave on tropical island to vacation in another? That's like leaving your bed to go sleep on the couch for no reason. Well as the case may be, I was visiting family, and while we were there we decided to go to the beach and it got me thinking quite a bit. You see, the beach holds a lot of memories for some people. Hanging out with friends, celebrating with family, romantic walks at night, many people have significant memories that are linked to the sea in some way or another. What memories does the ocean hold for you?


"I Wake"

October 03, 2024 by Nicole Johnson



I wake up in the morning to the same old scent
Of watered down coffee and the same old assortment
Of foods
That I know you like to do,
But I'm tired of these foods
And I'm tired of this same old arguement
(But really I think I'm tired of you).

I look back and I remember the good days,
The days we were younger and the days we would play
This game.
What game?
This game of cat and mouse
I'd chase you all through the house
And we'd laugh and be okay
Can we go back to being okay
Again?

I wake up at night chasing that same old scent
One I now realize left with you when you went,
Ahead,
And now I'm dead
Inside, you were my life
You were my light
And now I have to get through strife
Without you by
My side.
All because I thought our love was spent
When I was simply just content.


I wrote this poem while listening to a sad song. I don't remember what it was called or what exactly the lyrics said, but I remember the instrumental had a melancholy quality to it. It made me think of yearning, of wanting for something you cannot have or maybe something that was once yours and is now lost to you. I like sentimental songs and themes like this because they really pull me in and make me feel, make me appreciate the emotions I'm capable of feeling. What is your favourite kind oof music?
